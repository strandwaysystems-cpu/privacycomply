GA4 is bloated, privacy-invasive, and legally requires a cookie consent banner for EU visitors. These alternatives give you the data you actually need — without the compliance headache.

Why people are switching away from Google Analytics

Google Analytics 4 launched as a mandatory replacement for Universal Analytics in 2023. Since then, complaints from marketers, developers, and site owners have been consistent:

  • Complexity: GA4's event-based model, Explorations interface, and BigQuery dependency are a step change in complexity from UA. Most small business owners can't use it without a consultant.
  • Privacy and legal risk: GA4 transfers data to Google's US servers, which has led to rulings from EU data protection authorities in Austria, France, Italy, and Denmark that its use violated GDPR. Using GA4 for EU visitors requires explicit cookie consent and a compliant banner.
  • Data accuracy issues: GA4 uses modelling and sampling to fill in gaps from users who decline consent — meaning the numbers you see may be estimated, not real.
  • You're feeding Google's data machine: Every visitor to your site who accepts analytics cookies is adding to Google's advertising profile dataset. For many site owners, this is an ethical line they'd rather not cross.

The cookie banner problem with Google Analytics

Key fact: If you have EU visitors and run Google Analytics, you legally need a cookie consent banner — and GA4 must be blocked until visitors explicitly accept. Without this, you are in violation of GDPR regardless of your business location.

This creates a painful tradeoff: EU visitors who decline cookies (commonly 30–60% of EU traffic) become invisible in GA4. Your data understates real traffic, and you're paying for analytics that misses a significant chunk of your audience.

The cookieless alternatives below track 100% of visitors — including those who would have declined a cookie banner — because they don't use cookies or collect personal data in the first place.

Our verdict

For most websites — blogs, SaaS products, e-commerce stores, and content sites — Plausible Analytics is the clear winner. At $9/month you get accurate traffic data for unlimited sites, a fast lightweight script, and complete GDPR compliance without a cookie banner. The $9/month cost is less than most cookie consent tools, so you come out ahead financially while eliminating the compliance overhead entirely.

If budget is a hard constraint, Umami (self-hosted) gives you cookieless analytics for free. It requires a server and a few hours of setup, but the result is the same: accurate traffic data with no privacy law exposure.

If you need GA4-level feature depth — heatmaps, session recordings, full funnel analysis — Matomo is the only genuine replacement. Budget for the self-hosting cost and setup time, or accept that the cloud version is expensive.

Still need a cookie banner? If you use advertising pixels, embedded videos, or social tracking alongside your analytics tool, you'll still need consent management for those. See our cookie consent tool comparison or use our decision tree to check exactly which cookies on your site require consent.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use Google Analytics without a cookie banner?

No. Google Analytics 4 sets cookies and sends data to Google's servers in the US, which constitutes processing of personal data under GDPR. EU visitors must consent before GA4 fires. If you want analytics without a cookie banner, switch to a cookieless tool like Plausible Analytics, Fathom, or Umami.

Is Google Analytics 4 GDPR compliant?

GA4 can be used in a GDPR-compliant way, but it requires a cookie consent banner, Google Consent Mode v2 integration, a signed Data Processing Agreement with Google, and disclosure of data transfers to the US in your privacy policy. Several EU data protection authorities have previously ruled that Google Analytics violated GDPR due to US data transfers.

What is the best free Google Analytics alternative?

Umami and Matomo (self-hosted) are the best free alternatives — both are open-source and cookieless. Plausible has a 30-day free trial at $9/month and is the best paid alternative for most sites.

Do cookieless analytics tools still track visitors accurately?

Yes — often more accurately than GA4. Because cookieless tools don't require consent, they track 100% of visitors including those who decline cookie banners (who can represent 30–60% of EU traffic). GA4 with consent mode loses visibility on visitors who decline, meaning your GA4 data can significantly undercount real traffic.

Which Google Analytics alternative is best for Shopify?

Plausible Analytics has a native Shopify integration and tracks revenue, orders, and conversion rates without cookies. It works alongside Shopify's built-in analytics and removes the need for a cookie consent banner for analytics tracking.
