Iubenda vs Termly vs Osano: Complete Comparison Guide 2026

📅 Updated June 6, 2026 ⏱ 14 min read
Bottom line: iubenda offers the broadest feature set under one subscription — cookie consent, legal documents, accessibility tools, and whistleblowing management. Termly positions itself as an affordable all-in-one solution for small businesses. Osano emphasizes enterprise-grade privacy management with regulatory guarantees. Pricing starts at €4.99/month (iubenda), ~$10/month (Termly), and $199/month (Osano).

Choosing between iubenda, Termly, and Osano means weighing different approaches to privacy compliance. Each platform handles cookie consent banners and privacy documentation, but they differ in scope, pricing transparency, and how they support businesses of varying sizes.

Key Takeaways

  • iubenda provides the widest compliance scope with cookie consent, legal documents, accessibility, and DSAR tools in one subscription
  • Termly serves small businesses seeking affordable all-in-one compliance, while Osano focuses on enterprise privacy management with regulatory guarantees
  • Pricing transparency varies significantly, with iubenda and Termly publishing clear plans and Osano requiring custom quotes for most tiers

Core Features Overview

All three platforms cover the foundational elements of privacy compliance, but each prioritizes different capabilities within policy generation, consent management, and automation tooling. iubenda bundles the widest feature set under a single subscription, while Termly emphasizes U.S.-focused policy generators and Osano concentrates on enterprise privacy management workflows.

Policy Generation Capabilities

iubenda includes a privacy policy generator, cookie policy generator, and terms and conditions generator across most paid tiers. The policy generators support 27 languages and update automatically when regulations change. You can customize clauses for specific data processors, third-party integrations, and regional laws without drafting text from scratch.

Termly also offers policy generators for privacy policies, cookie policies, and terms of service. The templates focus on U.S. laws such as CCPA and CalOPPA alongside GDPR requirements. Customization options are more limited compared to iubenda's multi-language library, and the tooling skews toward small businesses operating primarily in North America.

Osano does not include built-in policy generators. Teams using Osano typically rely on separate vendors or legal counsel to draft privacy policies and terms of conditions, which adds coordination overhead and additional contracts to manage.

Consent Management Platform

All three vendors operate as consent management platforms (CMPs). iubenda's CMP is IAB-validated for the Transparency & Consent Framework (TCF 2.3) and certified by Google as a CMP Partner with Consent Mode integration. The platform includes geo-targeting, granular consent categories, and consent recovery tools designed to optimize opt-in rates without forcing compliance.

Termly provides a consent management solution with basic banner customization and geo-rules for GDPR and CCPA. The CMP lacks advanced features such as consent paywalls or built-in analytics for tracking banner performance against site metrics.

Osano's privacy management approach includes a CMP with stronger enterprise workflows — vendor risk assessments, internal privacy management dashboards, and integration hooks for larger compliance stacks. Setup typically requires developer time or professional services, making it less suitable for smaller teams without dedicated GRC resources.

Compliance Automation Tools

iubenda's compliance scanning includes an automated cookie scanner that inventories trackers across your domain, rebuilds the cookie list when scripts change, and flags unregistered third-party tags. The platform also bundles compliance solutions such as data subject access request (DSAR) workflow management, a register of processing activities (ROPA), whistleblowing intake, and an accessibility widget aligned with the European Accessibility Act.

Termly includes a cookie scanner as part of its compliance software offering but does not extend into DSAR automation, whistleblowing, or accessibility tooling. Teams needing those capabilities must contract additional vendors.

Osano focuses scanning and automation on cookie and tracker detection, vendor assessments, and consent record retention. The platform integrates with enterprise systems for internal privacy management but does not offer a native accessibility module or whistleblowing portal.

Cookie Consent Solutions and Banners

All three platforms deliver cookie consent banners with automated scanning and customization options, though they differ in depth of control and ease of setup.

Cookie Banner Customization

iubenda offers extensive customization through its Google-certified consent banner, supporting over 10 languages natively with options to adjust layout, colors, and messaging to match your brand identity. The platform provides pre-built templates that you can modify without coding knowledge.

Termly delivers customizable cookie consent banners through a guided setup process designed for beginners. You can adjust banner placement, styling, and text content through their dashboard, though the free plan limits some customization features. The Pro+ plan unlocks custom branding options for a more tailored appearance.

Osano provides consent banner customization through an intuitive interface, but users report that customization options are more limited compared to other platforms. The banners support basic color and text modifications, with advanced styling features gated behind higher-tier plans starting at $199/month.

Automated Cookie Scanning

iubenda includes an automated site scanner that detects cookies and trackers across your website, organizing them by category and maintaining an up-to-date cookie policy generator. The scanner runs regularly to catch new cookies as your site evolves, with first-party cookie mapping included.

Termly's cookie scanner automatically identifies and categorizes cookies on your site, blocking them until user consent is obtained. The free plan covers up to 10,000 monthly pageviews with unlimited scans available on paid tiers. The scanner integrates directly with the cookie consent manager for streamlined compliance.

Osano automates cookie detection and classification through regular scans, maintaining real-time updates in your dashboard. The scanner identifies cookies from third-party vendors and tracks changes, though some users note the scanning frequency is less customizable than competing platforms.

Cookie Consent Management

iubenda centralizes consent tracking through a unified dashboard where you manage user preferences, consent logs, and cookie policies. The platform integrates with Google Consent Mode and Tag Manager, storing consent records to demonstrate compliance with GDPR and CCPA requirements.

Termly combines its cookie consent banner with automatic blocking capabilities, ensuring cookies don't load until users provide consent. The platform includes built-in privacy policy and cookie policy generators, maintaining consent records accessible through your account dashboard. DSAR tools help you respond to user data requests.

Osano's consent management system tracks user preferences across sessions and provides detailed consent logs with timestamps. The platform automates consent enforcement and includes vendor risk monitoring, backed by their "No Fines, No Penalties" pledge covering regulatory fines up to $500,000.

Privacy Law Coverage and Compliance

All three platforms support GDPR and CCPA, but they differ in how deeply they handle specific requirements like ePrivacy Directive cookie rules, CPRA's sensitive data categories, and emerging laws like Brazil's LGPD.

GDPR and ePrivacy Directive

iubenda offers the most granular GDPR compliance tools among the three. It provides automated privacy policy generation that covers GDPR's Article 13 and 14 transparency requirements, plus built-in support for the ePrivacy Directive's cookie consent rules. The platform automatically updates policies when EU regulations change and includes consent receipts that log user choices with timestamps.

Termly handles GDPR basics well for small businesses. Its cookie consent banners block trackers before consent, meeting ePrivacy Directive standards. However, customization options are more limited compared to iubenda, and the free plan doesn't include all GDPR-required features like automatic policy updates.

Osano goes beyond standard GDPR compliance by adding vendor risk monitoring. This helps you track third-party processors required under GDPR Article 28. The platform also covers ePrivacy cookie consent requirements and backs its GDPR tools with legal assurance through its "No Fines, No Penalties" pledge up to $500,000.

CCPA and CPRA Support

All three platforms support CCPA compliance, including "Do Not Sell My Personal Information" disclosures and opt-out mechanisms. iubenda and Osano both updated their tools to handle CPRA's expanded requirements, including sensitive personal information categories and the right to limit use.

Termly offers CCPA-ready privacy policies and consent management at lower price points. The platform includes data subject access request (DSAR) workflows for handling California consumer rights requests. However, CPRA-specific features like sensitive data handling are less detailed than what Osano provides.

Osano stands out with dedicated CPRA compliance tools that categorize sensitive personal information automatically. Its DSAR automation handles the full range of California privacy rights, including correction and deletion requests.

LGPD and International Laws

iubenda leads in international privacy law coverage. The platform supports Brazil's LGPD, South Africa's POPIA, Canada's PIPEDA, and multiple other data protection frameworks. Policy generators include country-specific clauses, and the consent management system adapts to different legal requirements automatically.

Termly covers LGPD basics but focuses primarily on US and EU regulations. International law support is less comprehensive, making it better suited for businesses operating mainly in North America and Europe.

Osano provides LGPD compliance tools and tracks regulatory updates across multiple jurisdictions. The platform's vendor risk features help manage international data transfers required under LGPD Article 33 and similar provisions in other laws.

Platform Usability and Customization

Ease of Use and Setup

Termly delivers the most streamlined onboarding experience. You can deploy a cookie banner and generate privacy policies in minutes without technical knowledge. The interface guides you through each step with clear prompts.

iubenda requires slightly more configuration but compensates with a well-organized dashboard. You'll need to map your cookies and adjust settings manually, though the process remains straightforward for most users. The platform provides detailed documentation to help you navigate setup.

Osano sits between the two. The initial deployment is quick, but accessing advanced features like vendor risk monitoring demands more time. The dashboard layout is intuitive, though some users report that finding specific settings can take extra clicks compared to Termly's simplified navigation.

Multi-Language Support

Among these three platforms, iubenda leads with support for over 12 languages in its consent banners and policy generators. This makes it particularly valuable if you operate in multiple European markets or serve diverse audiences.

Termly offers multi-language support on paid plans, covering the most common languages including English, Spanish, French, German, and Italian. The free plan limits you to English-only banners.

Osano provides multi-language consent management as well, though the language count falls behind iubenda. You'll find adequate coverage for major markets, but smaller regional languages may not be available.

Templates and Personalization

iubenda provides the most granular customization options. You can adjust banner layout, button placement, color schemes, and messaging while maintaining compliance standards. Policy templates are modular, letting you add or remove clauses based on your specific data practices.

Termly focuses on simplicity with pre-built templates that work out of the box. Customization options exist but are more limited on the free plan. Paid plans unlock custom branding, advanced styling, and the ability to modify policy language.

Osano offers functional templates with moderate customization. You can adjust colors and basic layout elements, though users note that banner design options feel more restricted compared to iubenda's flexibility. The platform prioritizes compliance over extensive visual customization.

Starting Price Comparison

iubenda's entry-level paid plan costs €4.99/month (approximately $5.30) for up to 25,000 pageviews with no restrictions on users or domains. Termly requires an annual subscription with its paid tier starting around $10/month when billed annually. Osano's first paid tier starts at $199/month for 30,000 visitors, capped at 2 users and 3 domains.

The pricing models differ structurally. iubenda offers monthly billing with transparent tier progression, while Termly locks you into annual contracts. Osano combines a published entry price with custom enterprise quotes for mid-tier and advanced plans.

Free Plan Inclusions

iubenda's free plan includes a basic cookie banner and privacy policy generator for sites with limited traffic. The consent solution operates without branding removal or advanced customization, suitable for personal projects or pre-launch websites.

Termly provides a free tier that covers basic privacy policy generation and cookie scanning for small sites. The plan includes watermarked consent banners and limited document customization.

Osano offers a free plan focused on foundational cookie consent and basic privacy policy tools. Like competitors, the free version includes branding and restricts advanced features such as geo-targeting and consent analytics.

Paid Plans and Upgrades

iubenda's paid tiers progress from €4.99/month to €19.99/month (50K pageviews with complete legal documents and geo-targeting) to €79.99/month (150K pageviews with advanced documents, Mobile SDK, and white-labeling). Each tier removes specific limitations rather than gating entire feature categories.

Termly's paid plans scale based on pageview volume and feature depth, with costs increasing substantially for businesses needing multi-language support or advanced compliance automation. Annual billing remains mandatory across paid tiers.

Osano's progression moves from the $199/month entry point directly to custom enterprise pricing. Mid-sized businesses fall into a quote-based pricing gap between the starter tier and full enterprise packages, making budgeting less predictable than with published tier structures.

Risk Management, Monitoring, and Integrations

Each platform approaches compliance monitoring differently, with varying levels of vendor risk assessment and user preference management. iubenda and Osano provide more comprehensive ROPA capabilities, while Termly focuses on streamlined document generation with basic monitoring features.

Compliance Monitoring

Osano offers the most robust compliance monitoring system among the three platforms. It provides continuous scanning of your website to detect changes in tracking technologies and third-party services. The system automatically alerts you when new cookies or scripts are added that could affect compliance.

iubenda includes compliance monitoring through its scanner tool, which checks your website for cookies and generates reports on tracking activities. You can schedule regular scans to maintain awareness of your compliance status. The platform updates your consent solution automatically when new tracking technologies are detected.

Termly provides basic compliance monitoring with its cookie scanner that identifies tracking technologies on your site. The tool runs periodic scans and notifies you of changes, though the monitoring is less comprehensive than Osano's real-time approach. You'll need to manually review and update policies based on scan results.

Vendor Risk and ROPA

Osano stands out with its vendor risk management capabilities and ROPA (Record of Processing Activities) functionality. The platform maintains a database of third-party vendors and their data processing activities, automatically populating your ROPA based on technologies detected on your site. You can track data flows, processing purposes, and legal bases for each vendor.

iubenda offers ROPA generation as part of its higher-tier plans, allowing you to document processing activities and generate required records for GDPR compliance. The system connects your privacy policy selections to ROPA entries, reducing duplicate work.

Termly provides basic ROPA templates but lacks the automated vendor risk assessment features found in Osano. You'll need to manually maintain records of processing activities and vendor relationships.

Dashboards and Preference Centers

All three platforms offer consent dashboards, but with different capabilities. Osano provides a centralized dashboard showing consent rates, user preferences, and compliance metrics across multiple properties. The preference center allows visitors to manage their consent choices granularly, including options for withdrawing consent and accessing data subject rights.

iubenda's preference center integrates with its consent solution, giving users control over cookie categories and individual services. The dashboard displays consent statistics and helps you demonstrate compliance to regulators.

Termly includes a basic preference center where users can modify their cookie settings. The dashboard shows consent acceptance rates and policy view metrics, though it offers fewer analytics than Osano's enterprise-focused reporting. Termly does not currently offer dedicated whistleblowing management features, unlike some enterprise compliance platforms.

Support, Reviews, and Market Feedback

Customer Support Quality

iubenda provides email support and maintains an extensive knowledge base with detailed documentation. You can access priority support on higher-tier plans, though live chat is not available on all pricing levels.

Termly offers email support along with a help center containing guides and tutorials. The platform focuses on self-service resources, which works well for straightforward compliance needs but may require patience for complex issues.

Osano provides tiered support based on your subscription level. Enterprise customers receive dedicated account management and priority assistance. The platform includes a support portal and email-based ticketing system for handling technical questions.

User Reviews and Insights

According to recent comparison data, iubenda holds a rating of 8.6/10 among free compliance tools, making it the highest-rated option in that category. Termly follows closely with an 8.4/10 rating, while Osano receives an 8.5/10 score.

Users praise iubenda for its global privacy compliance capabilities and comprehensive legal document generation. Termly receives positive feedback for its budget-friendly pricing and straightforward interface, particularly among small businesses and freelancers. Osano users highlight the platform's robust features and enterprise scalability.

Advanced and Specialized Compliance Tools

Google Consent Mode v2

Termly and iubenda both offer full support for Google Consent Mode v2, which allows your website to adjust how Google tags behave based on user consent choices. This means Google Analytics and Google Ads can still collect aggregated data while respecting user privacy preferences.

iubenda is a Google-certified CMP with IAB validation, giving you verified compliance when running ads in the EU. The platform automatically maps first-party cookies and integrates directly with Google Tag Manager for seamless consent signal transmission.

Termly also supports Google Consent Mode v2 and provides cookie blocking capabilities that prevent tags from firing until users grant permission. The implementation works across all major website platforms without requiring custom code.

Osano doesn't emphasize Google Consent Mode v2 as prominently in its feature set. The platform focuses more on vendor risk management and cross-regulation compliance rather than specific ad-tech integrations.

HIPAA and Data Subject Requests

None of these three platforms are designed as primary HIPAA compliance solutions, since HIPAA requires technical safeguards that go beyond consent management. You'll need dedicated healthcare compliance tools if you handle protected health information.

All three platforms do handle Data Subject Access Requests (DSARs) effectively. iubenda provides a centralized dashboard where you can manage access, deletion, and portability requests from users exercising their privacy rights. Termly includes DSAR tools in its Pro+ plan. Osano offers the most comprehensive DSAR automation, tracking request status and automating workflows to ensure you meet regulatory deadlines.

Osano stands out with automated vendor risk monitoring that helps you assess third-party processors handling user data. This becomes critical when managing DSAR requests that span multiple systems and service providers.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which platform offers the most comprehensive GDPR and CCPA compliance features for a growing business?

iubenda delivers strong GDPR coverage with detailed clause libraries tailored to European data protection requirements and multi-language support across ten languages. The platform has been refined specifically for EU markets since 2011. CCPA coverage exists but remains less comprehensive than its European counterparts.

Termly focuses primarily on US regulations including CCPA and CPRA with robust support for American privacy frameworks. GDPR compliance is available but lacks the depth of European-native platforms.

Osano positions itself as an enterprise-grade solution with broad regulatory coverage spanning GDPR, CCPA, LGPD, PIPEDA, and additional frameworks. The platform targets mid-market and enterprise customers needing multi-jurisdiction support.

How do the pricing models compare across plans, add-ons, and pageview or domain limits?

iubenda charges annual subscriptions starting around €29 for basic privacy and cookie policies on a single site. The Ultra plan at approximately €99 annually adds terms of use and sale generators. Each additional website requires a separate subscription.

Termly offers a free tier with branded policies for one website and up to 100,000 monthly page views. Premium plans start at roughly $15 monthly or $170 annually, removing branding and expanding pageview limits.

Osano uses enterprise pricing that is not publicly listed and requires sales conversations. Costs generally exceed both iubenda and Termly but include advanced privacy operations tools, dedicated account management, and compliance workflow features designed for teams.

Which solution provides the strongest consent management capabilities, including IAB TCF support and GPC handling?

iubenda provides a consent management platform with built-in consent logging and a registry that stores proof of user preferences. IAB Transparency and Consent Framework support is available on higher-tier plans.

Termly includes consent management with automatic cookie scanning and user preference centers. The platform supports IAB TCF 2.2 on business plans and treats GPC signals as valid opt-out mechanisms under California law.

Osano offers a full-featured consent manager with granular controls for purposes, vendors, and IAB TCF compliance built into standard plans. GPC signal handling is automatic and configurable, with advanced preference management including cross-device sync and consent withdrawal workflows.

How easy is it to implement and customize cookie banners, privacy policies, and preference centers without developer support?

iubenda requires some technical configuration but provides plugins for WordPress, Shopify, and major CMS platforms that simplify deployment. The visual banner designer allows color and position customization.

Termly delivers the most accessible no-code experience among the three platforms. Automated cookie scanning identifies trackers, and the banner builder uses drag-and-drop controls. Non-technical users typically complete setup in under an hour.

Osano assumes moderate technical literacy with implementation requiring tag manager deployment or direct script installation. The interface targets compliance teams rather than solo entrepreneurs.

What level of automation and monitoring is available for cookie scanning, policy updates, and compliance reporting?

iubenda offers periodic cookie scanning on paid plans but does not run continuous automated scans. Policy updates happen when you modify your business practices through the generator. The platform sends email alerts for major legal updates.

Termly provides automatic cookie scanning that runs on schedules you define, detecting new trackers and flagging unclassified cookies. Compliance monitoring includes basic reports showing consent rates and user preferences over time.

Osano includes continuous cookie scanning with real-time detection of new tracking technologies. Compliance dashboards track consent metrics, data subject requests, vendor assessments, and regulatory deadlines. Automated reporting generates audit-ready documentation for privacy impact assessments and regulatory filings.

How do the integrations and tag manager compatibility differ for common marketing and analytics stacks?

iubenda integrates directly with Google Tag Manager, allowing automatic blocking of tags until consent is granted. Native connections exist for Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, and major advertising platforms. WordPress, Joomla, and Shopify plugins handle common CMS environments.

Termly supports Google Tag Manager and provides template tags for popular analytics and marketing tools. Direct integrations include Shopify, WordPress, Wix, and Squarespace. Custom integrations require JavaScript knowledge.

Osano offers the broadest integration ecosystem with pre-built connectors for enterprise marketing stacks including Salesforce, Marketo, Adobe Analytics, and segment.io. Tag manager compatibility extends to Google, Tealium, and Adobe Launch. API access enables custom integrations with internal systems and privacy workflow tools.
