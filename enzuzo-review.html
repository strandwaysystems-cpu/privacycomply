Enzuzo Review 2026: 10 Domains for $59/mo, Real DSAR Workflow

📅 Updated June 27, 2026 ⏱ 12 min read ⭐ 4.5 / 5

What is Enzuzo?

Enzuzo is a Canadian privacy compliance SaaS founded in 2019 and headquartered in Waterloo, Ontario — what the founders describe as "the Silicon Valley of Canada" on their about page. The company is bootstrapped with a single institutional backer, Celtic House Venture Partners, and is led by serial entrepreneur Mate Prgin, who previously exited two companies (Avvasi to NetScout, VideoLocus to LSI). Per GetLatka's 2025 data, the team is around six people running roughly $660K ARR — small relative to OneTrust but profitable and well-positioned in mid-market consent management.

The product is two halves bolted together cleanly: a consent management platform (cookie banner, automated cookie scanner, Google Consent Mode v2, Microsoft UET Consent Mode, IAB TCF 2.3, regional consent rules) and a data subject access request (DSAR) workflow (intake form, routing, audit trail, compliance reporting). Around that core sits a set of auto-updating policy generators — Privacy Policy, Terms of Service, EULA, Returns and Shipping policies — and native apps for Shopify and Webflow that competitors don't match.

Pricing — the 10-domain math

Verified from enzuzo.com/pricing as of June 2026:

  • Free — $0: 1 domain, 5,000 monthly visitors, 1 user. Standard (non-custom) Privacy Policy and Terms of Service, custom cookie banner, basic consent logs, basic API access, Google Consent Mode v2, 1 language, 3 DSARs/month.
  • Starter — $9/month monthly, $7/month annual: 1 domain, 5,000 monthly visitors. Custom legal policies (Privacy Policy, Terms, EULA, Shipping & Returns), 25+ languages, removed Enzuzo branding, automatic country detection, 10 DSARs/month.
  • Growth — $29/month monthly, $22/month annual: 4 domains, 10,000 monthly visitors. Adds basic geo-specific consent, IAB TCF support, full DSAR management with custom intake form and multi-lingual support, audit trail, compliance reporting, subdomain consent, Do Not Track, premium support.
  • Pro — $79/month monthly, $59/month annual: 10 domains, 30,000 monthly visitors, up to 5 users. Adds advanced geo-specific consent, unlimited DSARs, unlimited site traffic, Global Privacy Control, Pro Support, DSAR Audit Trail, DSAR Compliance Report.
  • Enterprise — custom pricing: Unlimited everything. Advanced Analytics, Advanced API, Headless Mode, A/B Testing on consent banners, dedicated success manager, shared Slack channel, white-glove onboarding, sandbox environments, full DSAR automation, agency multi-client workflows with white-label client questionnaires.

The multi-site math is where Enzuzo wins. At 10 domains, Enzuzo Pro costs $59/month annual — that's $5.90 per domain. The same 10 domains on CookieYes Basic at $10/month each would cost roughly $100/month with no DSAR workflow and no API, per Enzuzo's 2026 CookieYes pricing breakdown. On Termly Pro+ at ~$15/month per domain, 10 sites would cost roughly $150/month. On Cookiebot's per-page-tier model, an agency managing 10 client sites at the Premium Medium tier pays approximately €300–€340/month before any sites exceed 3,500 pages, per Enzuzo's January 2026 Cookiebot analysis.

The flat-rate model breaks even at two domains versus per-site competitors and becomes structurally cheaper at 5+ sites. For PrivacyComply.io readers running portfolio sites, that's the headline.

Google Consent Mode v2 and IAB TCF

Enzuzo is a Google CMP Partner with Gold tier certification, listed on the Google CMP Partner Program page and registered as CMP ID 418 in Google Ad Manager's certified CMP list. Google Consent Mode v2 is supported on every plan including Free. Microsoft UET Consent Mode is also supported across all plans, per Enzuzo's Microsoft Consent Mode integration guide.

For IAB TCF, Enzuzo is a registered TCF 2.3 vendor — TCF support activates at the Growth tier and above. The platform implements TCF v2.2 and v2.3 via the TC String and Global Vendor List, per Enzuzo's CMP documentation.

The caveat is worth being honest about. Independent technical analysis from DataCops' 2026 CMP review describes Enzuzo's TCF publisher depth as "partial" rather than full publisher TCF stack — adequate for most ad-tech scenarios but not the right choice for heavy publishers running complex programmatic ad stacks that require full publisher TC String control. Most multi-site operators reading this review won't hit that ceiling. EU programmatic publishers should evaluate Cookiebot or Didomi instead.

Advanced Consent Mode: why Gold-tier certification matters for revenue

Gold-tier CMP certification isn't a marketing badge — it specifically signals support for Advanced Consent Mode (versus Basic). The difference is consequential. Under Basic Consent Mode, Google tags stay fully blocked until a visitor actively grants consent; if they decline, you lose the conversion event entirely. Under Advanced Consent Mode, supported natively by Gold-tier partners like Enzuzo, CookieYes, and Usercentrics, tags fire on page load and send stateless, cookieless pings to Google. When consent is denied, Google's conversion modeling reconstructs the missing data using machine learning — recovering a meaningful share of the conversion signal that Basic mode would have thrown away. For ad-driven sites this is the difference between bleeding measurement data and keeping GA4/Ads attribution intact.

The DSAR workflow — Enzuzo's quiet advantage

Data Subject Access Requests are the part of GDPR that most cookie consent tools quietly ignore. Termly's DSAR feature is a contact form. CookieYes has no DSAR workflow at all. iubenda includes a basic DSAR module on higher tiers. Enzuzo built a real one — and made it available at the $22/month Growth tier.

  • Free: 3 DSARs/month, basic intake only, no custom form, no reporting
  • Starter: 10 DSARs/month, DSAR reporting available, no custom form
  • Growth: 20 DSARs/month, custom DSAR intake form, multi-lingual DSAR, audit trail, compliance report
  • Pro: Unlimited DSARs, all Growth features
  • Enterprise: Full DSAR automation — identity verification, routing to data owners, fulfillment across GDPR, CCPA, and LGPD

For a site owner who has never received a data subject request, this matters more than it looks. GDPR Article 15 gives EU residents a 30-day response window when they ask for a copy of their personal data, and the wrong answer is a regulatory complaint. Having a real intake form, routing, and an audit trail in place before the first request arrives is the difference between answering it in an hour and scrambling for a week.

Integrations and platform support

Enzuzo deploys via native apps on the platforms its target buyer actually uses:

  • Shopify: Native app on the Shopify App Store built on Shopify's Customer Privacy API, launched June 2020. Enzuzo claims to be the only CMP in its competitive set with this depth of Shopify integration — OneTrust, Cookiebot, and Didomi do not have a comparable native Shopify app, per Enzuzo's ecommerce documentation.
  • Webflow: Native marketplace app on the Webflow App Marketplace — handles cookie scanning, banner display, and consent logging without custom code.
  • WordPress / WooCommerce: Supported via embed script.
  • Squarespace, Wix, BigCommerce, GoHighLevel: Supported via JavaScript embed.
  • Google Tag Manager: Full native integration — deploys consent initialization trigger before any tags fire, the Google-recommended deployment pattern. See Enzuzo's GCM implementation guide.
  • API: Basic API on all tiers including Free; Advanced API and Headless Mode on Enterprise only. Use cases include programmatic domain provisioning, consent retrieval, DSAR data retrieval, and server-rendered platforms.
  • Slack: Shared Slack channel for priority support is Enterprise-only.

Regulatory coverage

Enzuzo's regulatory coverage is broad. Verified across its homepage and the Security Stack vendor profile:

  • EU/UK: GDPR, UK GDPR, ePrivacy Directive — opt-in consent, cookie scanning, DSAR, right to erasure
  • United States: CCPA/CPRA, VCDPA (Virginia), CIPA (Colorado/Connecticut), and other US state laws with per-state targeting
  • Canada: PIPEDA federally and Quebec Law 25 specifically — relevant for Canadian operators given Quebec's stricter standalone consent regime
  • Brazil: LGPD
  • Australia: Privacy Act references in compliance materials

The auto-updating policy generators handle the writing side of compliance. As privacy laws evolve, Enzuzo pushes updates to deployed policies — a feature that's table-stakes in 2026 but missing from any static "free privacy policy generator" out there.

Counter-balance from Shopify reviewer apol: "Incredible support! I had some additional wishes to modify my cookie banner and I experienced 1:1 support for 2 days where every single one of my individual requests was met." Support quality appears to vary by tier and case.

Enzuzo vs the alternatives

Head-to-head with the tools we cover:

  • vs CookieYes: CookieYes has a more generous single-site free tier (more banner views) and a cleaner UI for single-domain operators. Enzuzo wins decisively for 2+ sites because CookieYes charges per domain at every tier — at 10 sites you'd pay roughly $100/month on CookieYes Basic with no DSAR workflow and no API, per Enzuzo's 2026 pricing analysis.
  • vs Termly: Termly wins on attorney-drafted US legal document breadth (10 generators including disclaimer, EULA, return policy) and on US state law depth. Enzuzo wins on multi-domain pricing, native Shopify integration, and the real DSAR workflow. For a single US-focused site that primarily needs legal documents, Termly is still the right pick. For 4+ sites or any Shopify store, Enzuzo wins.
  • vs iubenda: iubenda's plans run from €4.99/month to €79.99/month and are billed per site per iubenda's own comparison page. iubenda wins on EU legal pedigree and guided onboarding. Enzuzo wins on flat-rate multi-domain pricing and DSAR depth. See our full three-way comparison.
  • vs Cookiebot: Cookiebot has deeper IAB TCF integration and stronger scanning accuracy for high-traffic publishers running programmatic ads, but its per-domain-and-per-page-tier pricing punishes multi-domain operators. Enzuzo's January 2026 Cookiebot analysis calculates that an agency managing 10 client sites at Cookiebot's Premium Medium tier pays €300–€340/month. Enzuzo Pro covers the same 10 domains for $59/month annual.
  • vs OneTrust: Not a comparison most readers of this review will make — but worth noting. Per Enzuzo's OneTrust pricing analysis and corroborating reporting from CheckThat.ai and DataCops, OneTrust raised its minimum annual contract value to roughly $10,000/year in Q2 2026 — the entry-level Consent and Preference Essentials module alone runs around $827–$1,100/month for a single domain. That priced out essentially every SMB and most mid-market teams using OneTrust purely for cookie consent. Enzuzo positions itself as "up to 80% cheaper than OneTrust" — true, with the caveat that OneTrust is a full GRC platform and Enzuzo is a focused consent and DSAR tool.

Who should buy Enzuzo in 2026

Good fit

  • Multi-domain operators running 4–20 websites — agencies, portfolio site owners, freelancers managing client compliance. The 10-domain Pro plan is structurally the best deal on the market at this tier.
  • Shopify merchants — the native Shopify Customer Privacy API integration is materially better than embed-script alternatives from Termly, OneTrust, Cookiebot, or Didomi.
  • Webflow site owners — same logic; native Webflow marketplace app vs. custom code injection on competitors.
  • Canadian operators — explicit Quebec Law 25 and PIPEDA coverage from a Canadian company that understands the regulatory context.
  • Anyone who actually expects to receive DSARs — Growth tier at $22/month is the cheapest real DSAR workflow on the market.
  • Site owners considering OneTrust who realized the minimum annual contract is $10,000+ — Enzuzo's mid-market tier hits the same compliance bar without the enterprise contract.

Bad fit

  • Single-domain solopreneurs who only need legal documents and a basic banner. Termly's free tier and attorney-drafted legal library are stronger here.
  • High-traffic single-domain sites — Starter caps at 5,000 monthly visitors, Growth at 10,000, Pro at 30,000. Above that you'd negotiate Enterprise pricing or look at Cookiebot's per-page tier model.
  • EU publishers running heavy programmatic ad stacks — the partial TCF publisher depth is a real constraint. Cookiebot or Didomi are the right picks.
  • Enterprise procurement requiring SOC 2 or ISO 27001 documentation — neither is publicly available as of June 2026.
  • Operators who can't tolerate any billing reliability risk — the Shopify double-billing complaint is a single review but it's worth knowing about before you sign up via the Shopify install path versus direct.

The verdict

Enzuzo is the right tool for the buyer who has outgrown single-site consent management and isn't ready to write a $10,000 check to OneTrust. The Pro plan's 10-domain economics, the genuine DSAR workflow from $22/month, the native Shopify and Webflow apps, and the verified Google CMP Gold status add up to a defensible recommendation for the mid-market.

The weaknesses are real and worth knowing. Visitor caps on lower tiers constrain higher-traffic single-domain sites. The partial publisher TCF depth makes Enzuzo a poor fit for heavy programmatic publishers. The recurring billing reliability complaint on the Shopify App Store is a single review but a serious one — if you go this route, install via Enzuzo direct rather than the Shopify App Store path and keep an eye on the first three months of statements. The lack of public SOC 2 documentation is a real gap for enterprise procurement.

The 4.5/5 rating reflects: structurally best multi-domain pricing in the mid-market, real DSAR workflow at an entry-level price, native Shopify and Webflow apps, Google CMP Gold status, IAB TCF 2.3 registration, and explicit Quebec Law 25 support — offset by visitor caps, partial publisher TCF depth, the Shopify billing reliability concern, and missing public security certifications.

Final recommendation

Enzuzo Pro — the multi-site default

If you run 4+ websites, the Pro plan at $59/month annual is the cheapest defensible way to run cookie consent and DSAR workflows across the portfolio in 2026. Start with the Free tier on your highest-traffic site to test the banner and policy generator, then upgrade to Growth or Pro when you need geo-targeting and DSAR management.

FAQ

Is Enzuzo cheaper than CookieYes or Termly for multiple sites?

Yes — dramatically. At 10 domains, Enzuzo Pro costs $59/month annual. The same 10 domains on CookieYes Basic at $10/month each would cost roughly $100/month with no DSAR workflow and no API. On Termly Pro+ at ~$15/month per domain, 10 sites would cost roughly $150/month. The flat-rate model breaks even at two domains and becomes structurally cheaper at 5+ sites.

Does Enzuzo support Google Consent Mode v2?

Yes. Enzuzo is a Google CMP Partner with Gold tier certification (CMP ID 418 in Google Ad Manager's certified list). GCM v2 is supported on every plan including Free. Microsoft UET Consent Mode is also supported across all plans. IAB TCF activates at the Growth tier and above.

Does Enzuzo have a real DSAR workflow?

Yes — and this is one of Enzuzo's clearest advantages. The Growth tier ($22/month annual) includes a custom DSAR intake form, multi-lingual DSAR support, audit trail, and compliance reporting. Pro removes the monthly DSAR cap. Termly's DSAR is form-only with no routing; CookieYes does not offer a DSAR workflow at all.

Is Enzuzo a good fit for Shopify stores?

Yes — Enzuzo has a native Shopify app built on Shopify's Customer Privacy API, available on the Shopify App Store since June 2020. OneTrust, Cookiebot, and Didomi do not have an equivalent native Shopify integration. The caveat: at least one Shopify reviewer has reported a two-year window of double-charges from the Shopify install path and Enzuzo direct billing running simultaneously. If you choose Enzuzo for Shopify, install via one path or the other — not both — and audit the first three months of statements.

What about non-Shopify, non-Webflow sites?

Enzuzo supports WordPress, WooCommerce, Squarespace, Wix, BigCommerce, GoHighLevel, and any platform that accepts a JavaScript embed snippet. Google Tag Manager integration is native and recommended for non-CMS sites.

What are Enzuzo's real weaknesses?

Three to consider: (1) visitor caps on lower tiers (5,000/month on Starter, 10,000/month on Growth, 30,000/month on Pro) constrain higher-traffic single-domain sites; (2) partial IAB TCF publisher depth is fine for most scenarios but not ideal for heavy programmatic publishers — Cookiebot or Didomi are stronger here; (3) recurring billing reliability complaints in Shopify reviews, including one verified report of two years of double-charges. For US-focused single-site solopreneurs who only need legal documents, Termly's attorney-drafted library is also worth comparing.

Is Enzuzo SOC 2 or ISO 27001 certified?

Not publicly confirmed as of June 2026. Neither certification appears on Enzuzo's public security pages. For enterprise procurement that requires SOC 2 or ISO 27001 documentation, you'd need to request status directly from Enzuzo or evaluate Cookiebot, Didomi, or OneTrust instead.
