What is CookieYes?

CookieYes is a consent management platform (CMP) used by over 1.5 million websites worldwide as of 2026. It puts a configurable banner on your site that asks visitors which categories of cookies they consent to — analytics, marketing, functional — and then blocks the matching trackers until consent is given.

Under the hood, CookieYes does three things: it scans your site for cookies and tracking scripts, it shows a banner that records each visitor's choices, and it integrates with Google's Consent Mode v2 so your analytics and ad pixels respect those choices.

Setup experience

On WordPress, the install path is:

  1. Install the CookieYes plugin from the WP repo
  2. Click "Connect to CookieYes" — opens a sign-up flow
  3. Choose a banner template from the starter designs
  4. Run the initial cookie scan
  5. Toggle Google Consent Mode v2 on
  6. Save and publish

Realistic time from plugin install to a live, consent-respecting banner: under 10 minutes for WordPress, around 10–15 minutes for non-WordPress sites where you install the script tag manually (Shopify, Webflow, custom HTML).

Regulatory coverage

  • GDPR (EU + UK) — Full opt-in mode, granular categories, consent log export.
  • CCPA / CPRA (California) — Auto-switches to opt-out mode for US visitors with a "Do Not Sell My Personal Information" link.
  • PIPEDA (Canada) — Implied + express consent modes available. Note: Quebec Law 25 requires express consent only — make sure you switch the Quebec geo-rule on.
  • LGPD (Brazil) — Portuguese-language banner template, opt-in mode.
  • Australia Privacy Act — Not required by current law but supported preemptively.

What we liked

  • The free tier is actually free. No 14-day trial trick — you can run a banner indefinitely under 25k visits/month.
  • Geo-targeting works out of the box. EU visitors see opt-in GDPR mode, US visitors see opt-out CCPA mode, automatically.
  • Google Consent Mode v2 is one toggle. Several competitors require GTM template imports or custom JS for the same result.
  • The cookie scanner is thorough. CookieYes' own scanner inventories first- and third-party cookies on your domain so you can match each one to a consent category before going live — a step most banner tools skip.
  • Consent log export is included on the $10 plan. This is what you'll need if a regulator ever asks "show me proof of consent for visitor X on date Y."

Where it falls short

  • No privacy policy generator. You'll still need iubenda or Termly for that. CookieYes does generate a basic cookie policy, but not a full privacy policy.
  • Custom CSS requires the $25/mo Pro plan. The Basic plan limits you to template choices.
  • IAB TCF v2.2 certification on Pro tier only. If you run ad-tech inventory through Google AdSense or programmatic auctions, this is mandatory and worth the upgrade.
  • Multi-domain pricing is per-domain. Agencies managing 20+ client sites will find the cost adds up.

Who CookieYes is for

  • Small business owners with one website
  • Bloggers and content creators with EU traffic
  • Ecommerce stores on Shopify, WooCommerce, or BigCommerce

Frequently asked questions

Is CookieYes really free?
Yes — the free tier covers up to 25,000 page views per month with full GDPR, CCPA, PIPEDA, and LGPD support. It shows a small "Powered by CookieYes" label, which the $10/month plan removes.
Does CookieYes support Google Consent Mode v2?
Yes, natively. You enable it with one toggle in the CookieYes dashboard. It works with Google Analytics 4, Google Ads, and Google Tag Manager out of the box.
Does CookieYes work outside WordPress?
Yes. CookieYes ships a universal script tag that works on any HTML site — Shopify, Webflow, Squarespace, Framer, custom code. The WordPress plugin just makes the install slightly faster.
How much does CookieYes cost for a real business?
For most small businesses, $10/month (Basic) is enough — it removes branding and adds multi-language support. The $25/month Pro tier is needed if you require custom CSS, advanced consent logs, or more than three languages.
Is CookieYes GDPR-compliant on its own?
CookieYes provides a GDPR-compliant consent mechanism, but GDPR compliance for a website also requires a privacy policy, lawful basis for processing, and a data subject request process. CookieYes covers the visible consent layer well; you'll still need a privacy policy from iubenda, Termly, or your own legal counsel. Read our GDPR guide for the full compliance picture.
