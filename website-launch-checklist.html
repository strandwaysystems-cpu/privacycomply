Frequently asked questions

What legal pages does a website need before launch?

At minimum: a Privacy Policy (required under GDPR, CCPA, and most other privacy laws), a Cookie Policy (required if you use non-essential cookies), and Terms and Conditions (recommended for any commercial site). If you have EU visitors and use analytics or advertising cookies, you also need a cookie consent banner.

Do I need a cookie banner before launching my website?

Yes, if your site uses analytics, advertising pixels, or any third-party tracking scripts and you expect EU visitors. The cookie banner must be in place before launch — running tracking scripts before consent is in place is a GDPR violation from day one.

What is the most commonly missed item on a website launch checklist?

Cookie consent and privacy compliance. Most developers focus on technical performance and design QA, and legal requirements get added as an afterthought — often after traffic (and GDPR exposure) has already started.

How long does it take to make a website legally compliant?

With the right tools, under an hour. A cookie consent banner via CookieYes takes 15 minutes to install. A lawyer-vetted privacy policy via iubenda takes 10 minutes to generate. The most time-consuming part is auditing which cookies and tracking scripts your site actually uses.
